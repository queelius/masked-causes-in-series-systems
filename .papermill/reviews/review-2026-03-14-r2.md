## New Issues

### MAJOR (2)

#### M1. Theorem 8(b) proof requires right-censored observations not stated in conditions
**Source**: Logic checker (confidence 92)
**Location**: Theorem 8(b), lines 1094–1099 and proof Step 1

The proof's Step 1 constructs a right-censored observation to derive the total hazard equality (the all-ones row of C̃). The theorem conditions only require "positive probability to exact-failure events (type E)." If the mechanism doesn't produce right-censored observations, Step 1 fails. A fully-masked exact failure (c = {1,...,m}) cannot substitute: its log-likelihood is log f_T(t), which is tautological.

**Fix**: Add "and right-censored observations (type R)" to the theorem condition. This is a one-phrase fix — virtually all reliability studies have right-censoring.

#### M2. Theorem 9(c) bound q·d is incorrect without parameter sharing
**Source**: Logic checker (confidence 85)
**Location**: Theorem 9(c), lines 1260–1263

The bound "at most q·d identifiable parameters" assumes each super-component's aggregate hazard has d identifiable parameters. For Weibull components with distinct shapes within a super-component, the sum A·t^a + B·t^b has 4 identifiable parameters (2 shapes + 2 scales), not d = 2. The bound holds when components share parameter structure (e.g., common shape Weibull) but not in general.

**Fix**: Either qualify: "assuming components within each super-component share the same parametric structure," or replace with the correct general bound.
