## Verdict: Minor Revision

The paper is mathematically sound, well-organized, and achieves its stated goal as a foundational reference. The C1–C2–C3 derivation is correct and pedagogically excellent. The identifiability theorems (Theorems 8–9) are genuinely novel. Two issues require attention before the paper is final: a logical overclaim in Theorem 9(a) and a missing intellectual ancestor (Meilijson 1981). The remaining items are minor fixes.

## Issues by Severity

### MAJOR (2)

#### M1. Theorem 9(a) claims "if and only if" but proves only sufficiency
**Source**: Logic checker (high confidence)
**Location**: Theorem 9, Part (a), lines 1214–1218

Theorem 9(a) states that aggregate hazards are identifiable "if and only if" the reduced candidate-set matrix C̃* has full column rank q. The proof establishes only the sufficient direction (by reduction to Theorem 8(b)). The necessary direction is not proven and is actually false for families with linearly independent hazard functions, as Remark 6 (lines 1158–1173) explicitly acknowledges: separability alone suffices when hazards are linearly independent, even without full column rank.

**Fix**: Change "if and only if" to "if" (sufficient condition), or add the qualifier "for families with linearly dependent hazards (e.g., exponential)." This is a 2-word fix that resolves a logical error.

#### M2. Missing citation: Meilijson (1981) — intellectual ancestor of Theorem 8
**Source**: Targeted literature scout (high confidence), cross-verified by broad scout
**Location**: Section 5.6 (Identifiability), near Theorem 8

Meilijson (1981), "Estimation of the lifetime distribution of the parts from the autopsy statistics of the machine" (J. Applied Probability 18:829–838), proved that identifiability in the autopsy model depends on the rank of the incidence matrix of minimal fatal sets. This is structurally analogous to Theorem 8(b)'s condition on the rank of the augmented candidate-set matrix C̃. Nowik (1990) refined this to necessary and sufficient conditions for coherent systems.

The paper's identifiability theorem is a genuine contribution (different combinatorial object — candidate sets from diagnostics vs. minimal cut sets; different data model — masked vs. autopsy), but the algebraic approach (rank of an observation-type matrix ⟹ identifiability) has clear precedent. A knowledgeable reviewer will flag this omission.

**Fix**: Add Meilijson (1981) and optionally Nowik (1990) to refs.bib. Add a sentence in the identifiability section: "The algebraic approach — linking identifiability to the rank of an observation-type matrix — has precedent in the autopsy model for coherent systems \citep{meilijson1981}, where identifiability depends on the rank of the incidence matrix of minimal fatal sets. Our candidate-set matrix $\tilde{C}$ plays an analogous role in the masked-data setting."

### MINOR (10)

#### m1. Lin-1993 bib entry: wrong author order
**Source**: Citation verifier (high confidence)
**Location**: refs.bib, Lin-1993 entry

The `author` field lists Usher first: `{Usher, J.S. and Lin, D.K.J. and Guess, F.M.}`. IEEE Xplore confirms Lin is first author. `\citet{Lin-1993}` currently renders as "Usher et al. (1993)" instead of "Lin et al. (1993)".

**Fix**: Change to `{Lin, D.K.J. and Usher, J.S. and Guess, F.M.}`.

#### m2. Left/interval-censored likelihood: implicit assumption beyond C1–C2–C3
**Source**: Logic checker (high confidence)
**Location**: Section 5.3, "Censored observations" paragraph (lines 896–909)

Factoring β_i out of the integral for left/interval-censored observations requires that the masking probability does not depend on the unknown failure time t. This is physically reasonable (diagnostic at inspection time, not failure time) but is not formally captured by C1–C2–C3 as stated. C3 says β_i doesn't depend on θ, not that it doesn't depend on t.

**Fix**: Add one sentence acknowledging this: "This step uses the additional assumption that the diagnostic outcome depends on the inspection time rather than the unknown failure time, so that $\beta_i$ is constant with respect to the integration variable $t$."

#### m4. Equation~\ref inconsistency at line 1078
**Source**: Format validator (high confidence)

#### m5. Identifiability definition not stated before proof
**Source**: Prose auditor (confidence 85), corroborated by logic checker
**Location**: Before Theorem 8 proof (line ~1090)

The proof assumes L(θ) = L(θ') "for all data sets" without defining identifiability. A reader may wonder why it suffices to check contrived single-observation data sets.

**Fix**: Add before the proof: "Recall that $\v{\theta}$ is identifiable if $L(\v{\theta}; D) = L(\v{\theta}'; D)$ for all possible data sets $D$ implies $\v{\theta} = \v{\theta}'$."

#### m6. Pareto support statement is misleading
**Source**: Logic checker (high confidence)
**Location**: Lines 1614–1618

"The system support is $t \geq \max_j x_{\min,j}$" is incorrect — the system can fail at any $t \geq \min_j x_{\min,j}$. What is true is that the Pareto formula for $R_j(t)$ applies only for $t \geq x_{\min,j}$.

**Fix**: Change to: "For $t < x_{\min,j}$, component $j$ cannot fail ($R_j(t) = 1$, $h_j(t) = 0$); the Pareto hazard formula applies only for $t \geq x_{\min,j}$."

#### m7. hyperref loaded before natbib
**Source**: Format validator (medium confidence)
**Location**: paper.tex lines 6, 9

Non-standard load order. Works on current TeX Live but may fail on stricter distributions.

**Fix**: Swap lines 6 and 9.

#### m8. $S_i$ vs $s_i$ notation ambiguity
**Source**: Prose auditor (confidence 75)
**Location**: Section 4.4 (line 563) vs notation table

The text uses $S_i$ (random variable) in the dependency figure description but the notation table only defines $s_i$ (realization). The random variable / realization convention is never stated.

**Fix**: Add one sentence in Section 4.2: "We use uppercase ($S_i$, $\mathcal{C}_i$) for random variables and lowercase ($s_i$, $c_i$) for their observed realizations."

#### m9. \footnotemark/\footnotetext float risk in Table 4
**Source**: Format validator (high confidence)
**Location**: Lines 1599, 1608

The Gamma footnote uses `\footnotemark`/`\footnotetext` split across the table float boundary. If page layout changes, the footnote may land on a different page than the table.

**Fix**: Move the footnote text into the table caption.

#### m10. Missing references from prior-art survey (Priority 3)
**Source**: Both literature scouts, novelty assessor
**Location**: Related work section, identifiability section

Several works identified by the scouts that would strengthen the paper:
- **Crowder (2001)**, *Classical Competing Risks* — standard textbook, reviewers expect it
- **Heckman & Honore (1989)**, Biometrika — covariates restore identifiability
- **Mukhopadhyay (2006)**, J. Stat. Planning & Inference — general MLE without C2
- **Geskus (2024)**, Annual Review — major recent competing risks review

None are critical omissions, but Crowder (2001) and Meilijson (1981) together would significantly strengthen the identifiability section's scholarly context.

### SUGGESTIONS (3)

#### S1. Reframe introduction to lead with novel contributions
**Source**: Prose auditor (confidence 90), novelty assessor

The passage "this paper does not claim the C1–C2–C3 conditions or the basic likelihood derivation as novel contributions" (line 164) is intellectually honest but rhetorically self-defeating. Theorems 8–9 (identifiability, confounding graph) ARE genuinely novel. Lead with these rather than the disclaimer.

#### S2. Consolidate "What breaks without C_k" paragraphs
**Source**: Prose auditor (confidence 70)

The three "What breaks" paragraphs (Sections 5.1–5.3) make essentially the same point (masking distribution remains in the likelihood). Consider a single consolidated remark after C3.

#### S3. Add series system diagram
**Source**: Prose auditor (confidence 85), format validator

`image/series.tex` exists but is not included in the paper. A simple block diagram in Section 2 showing components in series would help readers unfamiliar with the model.
